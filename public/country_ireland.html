Study In Ireland

Information about Study in Ireland

Ireland, which is a member of the European Union, has one of the world's fastest-growing economies and a young population. Ireland is become a preferred choice among Indian students since it is consistently recognised among the top 20 nations in the world for great quality of life, peace, human development, and dynamic culture.

Admission Requirement

Students applying to study in Ireland can do so directly to universities or via the Central Applications Office for undergraduate programs. Indian students must demonstrate English language proficiency and submit required documents for admission.

  • Copy of a valid passport
  • Academic Transcript
  • ACT/SAT/LSAT for UG programs
  • GMAT/GRE scores for PG programs
  • Evidence of funds
  • English proficiency scores(IELTS/TOEFL)
  • Letter of recommendation
  • CV/Resume/Essays
  • Statement of purpose(SOP)

Education System

Comprehensive and Government-Funded Education Framework Ireland's higher education system includes universities, technological institutes, and colleges, mostly funded by the government. The National Framework of Qualifications (NFQ) ensures standardization and easy transfers.

  • Non-Degree Programs: Higher Certificate (2 years), Diploma (1-2 years)
  • Degree Programs: Bachelor’s (3 years), Master’s (1 year)

Careers and Employment Opportunities

Work While You Study and Stay BackInternational students can work part-time during term (20 hours/week) and full-time during holidays. Graduates can stay in Ireland for up to 12 months (6 months for ordinary Bachelor’s) to find employment. Key industries include software, pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, and international services.
