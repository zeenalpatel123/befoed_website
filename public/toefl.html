Overview

  • The TOEFL also referred to as the Test of English as a Foreign Language, is an evaluation tool for students' English language competency.

  • The TOEFL preparation programme makes use of the student's abilities in the test-relevant domains of Speaking, Listening, Reading, and Writing.

  • The test is given 30 to 40 times year and is recognised by all US educational institutions as well as several in Australia, New Zealand, the UK, and Canada.

  • The exam is scored out of a total of 120, with every section being scored between a scale of 0-30.

  • The TOEFL test score results are valid for a period of 2 years.
