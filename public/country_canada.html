Admission Requirement

Confidence comes from being prepared

Candidates must be aware of every step in the application process as there are various college intakes in Canada, each of which corresponds to a distinct academic term. Students must submit these documents regardless of their colleges or intakes.

  • A complete application form
  • Letter of recommendation
  • A Resume
  • Proof of English proficiency
  • Academic Transcript
  • Statement of purposes
  • Essays
  • Standardized test scores

Education System

World-Class Education Recognized Globally Canadian universities uphold high academic standards, making it easier for international graduates to secure employment.

  • Diploma/Advanced Diploma: Includes co-op work terms for real-world experience (2-3 years).
  • Bachelor’s Degree: Awarded after 3-4 years of study; a pathway to graduate-level studies.
  • Post-Graduate Diplomas/Certificates: Require a Bachelor’s degree or diploma.
  • Master’s Degree: For advanced knowledge and professional growth; typically 1-2 years.
  • Doctorate (PhD): Higher-level research-focused degree; takes 3-7 years to complete.

Careers and Employment Opportunities

Work While You Study

  • Students with a study permit at public universities can work up to 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during breaks without needing a separate work permit.
  • Graduates may be eligible for the Post-Graduation Work Program (PGWP), allowing them to work in Canada for up to 3 years after graduation.
